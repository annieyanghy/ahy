Problem

2020 is a difficult year. When working from home and isolated from normal social interaction, people suffer from mental issues to different degrees.

Starting from ourselves, as a group of 4, we unanimously agreed that we want to observe what we can improve during the work from home life.

Solution

A smart assistant device that recreates the subtle and random social interaction by connecting people in a virtual space and prompting them to chat.

How It Works

1. Talkie senses user when user walks into a room.

2. Users can become friends with others on the device, which makes the device connected.

3. If the user's status is online and there are other friends online, it will prompt users to talk to others.

Research

Key insights
3 factors to influence sustainable behaviors: Convenience, Reduce living cost & guilt, and Social or environmental support.

Each of us took picture of our working environment at home and documented our mood and thoughts for one day.


2. User Interview

We conducted 8 semi-structured interviews to understand the pain point of our target users' work-from-home life.

3. Design criteria

We developed the following design criteria based on previous user feedback. From the previous research, we found users' preference and concerns when using such a device that can sense their motion and presence at home. As a result, the following criteria are the key principle we had in mind when iterated the idea.

Privacy

Talkie should beware if it is intruding on users' privacy.

Social Balance

Talkie should consider helping users in balancing their social life and personal time.

Feedback

Talkie should inform users of important information through feedback, e.g. current status, are they detectable, etc.

Social Norms

Talkie should avoid putting users into an awkward social situation or causing social pressure to them.

Price

Talkie should not be de too costly, so it will be easier to be adopted by the users.


4. Low-fi prototype 1: User Enactment

With the previous research result, we synthesized a few different scenarios to use the product. We then recruited another 5 users to enact the scenarios to observe their most natural reactions.

Since our product includes voice command, I used google translate and Wizard of Oz during the process to enact the scene.
