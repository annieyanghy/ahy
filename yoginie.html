Problem

People who are busy or travel a lot have trouble go to a local yoga studio due to their schedule. However, it's also difficult to find online yoga classes with good quality and customisable sessions to fit their tight schedule.

I had the idea since 2015 when I solo travelled widely in Europe. Travellers would not want to miss any sightseeing spot, neither did I. I tried to keep a daily schedule as tight as possible but still wanted to squeeze a quick but nice Yoga practice in the morning right before hectic adventures. Sadly, I could not find any mobile app that provides proper yoga guidance which can be customized into different duration and styles to suit my schedule.

Solution

A customizable yoga app that can provide tailored duration and types of yoga.

A few years later, I not only completed my 200 hours Yoga Teacher Training but also have the idea to make an app to help more busy on-the-road people to involve more Yoga practice.

Research

1. User Interview

To empathise more with the users, I started the user research with user feedback interview and my understanding of the culture yogis.

In the User Feedback Interview, I divided the interview into three parts. First, I built the imaginary use case for them and asked them about their past travelling experiences and workout pattern. Second, I showed them the app “Yoga Studio” and observed their behaviour. Last, I showed them my paper prototype, observed how they interacted with my paper prototype, and asked their expectation.

Design

User Interface Design

I designed the User Interface of the app, and the main colour is the blue with HEX #B6DCE3. I want to convey a concept of harmony and elegance. Harmony and elegance are also the two emotions I wish all of the users could feel the same way about themselves when or after using Yoginie.


Visual Design

I chose blue, grey and beige as primary colours because blue brings calmness and beige is a neutral and naked colour, which is the way I believe how yoga transforms people—leading people to the purest self.

The inspiration of the logo is “Flower of Life” (the left one). Flower of Life is a token composing multiple evenly-shaped and overlapping circles, which are arranged in a flower-like pattern in a hexagon. The logo has been regarded as a sacred geometry for centuries. With its harmoniously shaped circles, “Flower of Life” delivers messages of the endless life cycle and the harmonious energy pattern on the Earth.

Inspired by Flower of Life, I use four octagons to shape the logo, and three lines form the letter "Y", which means "Yoginie". I choose geometrical shape as the base of the logo to deliver the idea of natural harmony. I wish every user would receive a sense of harmonious beauty upon entering the app. Besides, I chose to integrate the blue and beige in the logo to create a message of calm and peace.

Takeaway

How to avoid discrimination

When I was asking feedback from my peers, one of the feedback I got from my peers is the importance to avoid discrimination through the profile picture, so I was seriously considering replacing profile picture with avatars.

However, there was no sufficient research to support this theory and I faced the difficulty to implement it in the high-fidelity design. One of the problems is due to my lack of ability to visualize the idea in the prototype. Also, the other one is that I started to doubt whether it helps to swap a human-face picture with an avatar, which can be further validated through A/B testing later on, from my point of view. As a result, I decided to adopt a human-face profile picture first and then have the avatar version as A/B testing.
